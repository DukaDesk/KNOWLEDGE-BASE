# Analytics Domain

## Purpose

The Analytics domain is responsible for collecting, processing, analyzing, and presenting data-driven insights across the DUKADESK platform.

Analytics provides the foundation for operational intelligence, business reporting, behavioral analysis, and AI-assisted decision support.

No other domain may own or redefine the analytics data model, event taxonomy, metric definitions, dashboard framework, or insight generation.

---

## Responsibilities

The Analytics domain owns:

• Metrics

• Events

• Dashboards

• Reports

• Insights

• Data Collection

• Data Processing

• Data Retention

• Analytics Catalog

• Insight Generation

---

## Objectives

The Analytics domain ensures that:

• Platform and business events are collected consistently.

• Metrics are defined once and reused across the platform.

• Dashboards and reports provide accurate, timely information.

• Insights are generated responsibly and transparently.

• Analytics respects tenant isolation and privacy rules.

• Analytics remains independent of operational execution.

---

## Domain Concepts

### Event

Represents an observable occurrence within the platform.

Events may originate from any domain and are collected for analysis.

---

### Metric

Represents a quantifiable measurement of platform or business activity.

Metrics are defined, computed, and governed by the Analytics domain.

---

### Dashboard

Represents a visual presentation of metrics, reports, and insights.

Dashboards may be tenant-specific, role-specific, or executive-level.

---

### Report

Represents a structured output of analytical data.

Reports may be scheduled, ad hoc, or generated on demand.

---

### Insight

Represents an analytical conclusion, recommendation, or anomaly.

Insights may be generated by rules, machine learning, or AI models.

---

### Analytics Catalog

Represents the registry of metrics, events, dimensions, and reports.

The catalog ensures consistency and discoverability.

---

### Data Retention

Represents policies governing how long analytics data is stored.

Retention must comply with privacy, regulatory, and cost requirements.

## Business Rules

Every event must have a defined schema.

Every metric must have a single definition.

Events must be attributable to a domain and tenant.

Metrics must be computed consistently across tenants.

Dashboards must respect user and tenant permissions.

Reports must preserve historical accuracy.

Insights must be explainable and auditable.

Sensitive data must be excluded or anonymized in analytics.

Retention policies must be enforceable and auditable.

## Dependencies

Analytics depends on Identity for user context and permissions.

Analytics depends on Tenant Management for tenant isolation and configuration.

Analytics consumes events from all business domains and Platform Core.

Analytics may depend on Integrations for external data sources and visualization tools.

Analytics may feed insights into Administration, Commerce, and Customer Management.

## Architectural Principles

Analytics observes; it does not execute.

Events are contracts between domains and analytics.

Metrics are defined once.

Insights must be explainable.

Tenant data must be isolated.

Privacy and consent govern analytics data.

No domain may redefine analytics concepts.

Analytics must remain decoupled from transactional systems.

---

## Traceability

All implementation related to metrics, events, dashboards, reports, insights, data collection, and data retention SHALL trace back to the Analytics domain.

No implementation repository may redefine the Analytics model.
